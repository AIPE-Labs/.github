# Fulong Li

Researcher in power electronics, focused on AI-assisted design automation for converters, devices, magnetics, simulation, and energy systems.

I build open engineering workflows that connect power electronics expertise with modern AI agents: from topology exploration and component selection to loss modeling, thermal analysis, simulation debugging, and research-paper drafting.

## AIPE: AI for Power Electronics

AIPE is my collection of tools, datasets, skills, and reference projects for accelerating power electronics development with artificial intelligence.

The goal is practical engineering assistance rather than generic chat: AI workflows should understand converter topologies, semiconductor devices, magnetics, thermal limits, control loops, simulation tools, and validation planning.

## What AIPE Covers

- **Converter design automation**: topology recommendation, parameter calculation, efficiency estimation, cascade design, DAB, LLC, buck, boost, flyback, forward, SEPIC, Cuk, and related DC-DC workflows.
- **Device modeling and databases**: MOSFET loss prediction, conduction behavior, switching energy, thermal impedance, and power-device library management.
- **Optimization**: Pareto exploration for efficiency, power density, volume, loss, switching frequency, semiconductor area, ripple, and temperature constraints.
- **Magnetics**: inductor and transformer sizing, ferrite material references, core selection, Steinmetz loss estimation, PCB current sensors, and Rogowski coil designs.
- **Simulation agents**: reusable AI skills for PLECS, LTspice, SIMetrix/SIMPLIS, MATLAB/Simulink, DSP/FPGA control debugging, ANSYS, and COMSOL.
- **System-level energy applications**: DC microgrids, EV charging planning, energy-management agents, solid-state transformers, and bidirectional power conversion.
- **Research workflows**: IEEE-style paper planning, LaTeX manuscript generation, citation/evidence checks, and power-electronics knowledge bases.
